Modern Musical Terms

  • Auto-Tune — a technology for automatic pitch correction, used to fix inaccuracies in vocals or to create an artificial voice effect.
  • Sampling — the process of using parts of existing music recordings in a new context or work. This can be a snippet of melody, rhythm, or even voice.
  • DAW (Digital Audio Workstation) — software for creating, editing, and recording music. Examples: Ableton Live, FL Studio, Logic Pro.
  • Glitch — a style of music based on the use of digital errors and noise as a musical element, or the effect of digital errors in sound.
  • Mastering — the final stage of music production, where audio material is prepared for release, optimizing its loudness, frequency balance, and aligning tracks in an album.
  • Sidechain — a technique in sound production where one audio signal is used to control parameters of another (most often compression), for example, in dance music to enhance the beats.
  • Beatmaking — the process of creating the instrumental base of music, usually in hip-hop and electronic music, focusing on rhythmic beats and samples.
  • VST (Virtual Studio Technology) — a technology for creating software instruments and effects that integrate into DAWs for sound creation and processing.
  • Sound Design — the process of creating new sounds or altering existing ones using electronic means, often used in film, games, and electronic music.
  • Loop — a repeating musical fragment that plays cyclically. Loops can be rhythmic, melodic, or sound-based.
  • Drop — a part of a composition, often in electronic dance music, where there is a sudden change in rhythm or tempo after tension in the musical structure, usually eliciting an emotional peak in the listener.
  • Breakdown — a part of a composition, often in EDM, where the intensity of the music decreases before returning to the main rhythm or "drop."
  • Click Track — a metronomic sound used during recording or live performance to maintain tempo.
  • Stems — separate audio files (e.g., drums, vocals, synthesizers) that make up a complete musical composition. They are used for remixes or re-recording.
